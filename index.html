Nelson Mandela

The man who saved a billion lives


His major achievements:

  • 1964– Elected Honorary President of the Students' Union, University of Leeds Tyler Hopper

  • 1973– A nuclear particle discovered by scientists at the University of Leeds is named the "Mandela particle

  • 1975– Honorary life membership of the Students' Union, University of London

  • 1979– Awarded Honorary Doctorate of Law, University of Lesotho, Maseru, 29 September

  • 1980– August – Freedom of the City of Glasgow

  • 1983– Honorary citizenship of Rome, February

  • 1985– Revenue Staff Federation, United Kingdom, names its Commonwealth trade union scholarship after Nelson Mandela

  • 1986– Presented with the Alfonso Comin Foundation Peace Award in Barcelona, Spain

  • 1987– First person to receive the Freedom of the City of Sydney, Australia, 9 January.

  • 1988– Park in Montreal named "Parc Winnie-et-Nelson-Mandela" (Winnie's name removed in 1998)

  • 1989– Freedom of the Municipality of Kwekwe, Zimbabwe. Award received on his behalf by Oliver Tambo

  • 1990– Granted freedom of the City of Harare, Zimbabwe, March

  • 1991– Honorary LL.D Degree conferred, University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg, 6 September

  • 1992– Installed as Chancellor of the University of the North (now called University of Limpopo), 25 April
